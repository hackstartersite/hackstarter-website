1. Material: What is Hacking?

Hacking is the act of finding creative ways to manipulate systems — usually in ways they weren’t intended to be used. Sometimes it’s done to fix or test things. Other times, it’s done to break in or cause harm.

Types of Hackers

  • White Hat: Ethical hackers who help defend systems and find bugs before the bad guys do.
  • Black Hat: Hackers who attack systems illegally for money, data, or chaos.
  • Gray Hat: Hackers who operate in the gray zone — not always legal, but not always malicious either.

Real-World Analogy

Imagine a building with a locked door:

  • The white hat tests the door, reports any weakness, and helps the owner install a better lock.
  • The black hat tries to pick the lock and steal what’s inside.
  • The gray hat sneaks in, finds the weak lock, and tells the owner... but didn’t have permission to do it.
