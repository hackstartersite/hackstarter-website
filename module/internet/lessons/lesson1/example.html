2. Example Hack: Phishing

Meet Alex. They’re about to log into their favorite online game. But something’s not quite right...

Instead of the real login page, Alex lands on a page that looks almost identical — same logo, same layout, same username and password fields.

What’s happening?

This is a phishing attack — a fake login page designed to trick people into entering their real credentials.

How it works

  1. The hacker creates a perfect clone of the real login page
  2. They send it to Alex through an email, fake ad, or message
  3. Alex logs in — but their password goes straight to the hacker

How to spot it

  • Look at the URL — is it correct?
  • Check for HTTPS and a padlock symbol
  • Does the page feel off or slightly outdated?

Here's a comparison:

  • Real: https://gamezone.com/login
  • Fake: https://login.gamez0ne.net

We will be covering phishing more in a separate module as it is one of the most common and successful attack types in the real world. For now, understanding the concept is good enough.
